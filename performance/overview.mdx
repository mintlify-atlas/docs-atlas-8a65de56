Frappe HR's performance management module covers the full appraisal lifecycle — from goal setting and peer feedback to final score calculation and salary revision. All activity is organized inside a time-boxed **Appraisal Cycle**, giving HR managers a single place to track progress across the organization.

## How the appraisal cycle works

The diagram below shows how each document relates to the others inside one cycle.

```
Appraisal Cycle
  └── Appraisee list  (employees + their Appraisal Templates)
        └── Appraisal  (one per employee)
              ├── KRA vs Goals  (goal completion → goal score)
              ├── Self Appraisal  (employee self-ratings)
              └── Feedback  (reviewer ratings → avg feedback score)
                        └── Final Score  (average of the three scores)
```

<Steps>
  <Step title="Configure KRAs and templates">
    Create **KRA** records that define the key result areas relevant to your business. Then build an **Appraisal Template** that maps KRAs to weightages and lists the rating criteria used for self-appraisal and feedback.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create an Appraisal Cycle">
    Open **Appraisal Cycle**, set the **Cycle Name**, **Start Date**, **End Date**, and pick a **KRA Evaluation Method** (automated from goal progress, or manual rating). Use **Get Employees** to populate the appraisee list, then click **Create Appraisals**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Employees set goals">
    Each employee creates **Goal** records linked to the cycle and a KRA. Goals can be nested — a parent goal rolls up progress from its children automatically.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Run appraisals">
    HR or managers open each **Appraisal** document. Goal scores are calculated from linked goal progress. Employees complete the **Self Appraisal** tab. Reviewers submit **Employee Performance Feedback** documents.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Calculate and submit">
    The **Final Score** on the appraisal is the average of the **Goal Score**, **Self Appraisal Score**, and **Average Feedback Score** (or a custom formula set on the cycle). Submit all appraisals, then click **Complete Cycle** on the **Appraisal Cycle**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Score components

| Component | Source | Field on Appraisal |
|---|---|---|
| Goal Score | Average goal completion across linked KRAs | `total_score` |
| Self Appraisal Score | Weighted ratings from the employee | `self_score` |
| Average Feedback Score | Average `total_score` of submitted feedback docs | `avg_feedback_score` |
| **Final Score** | Average of the three above, or custom formula | `final_score` |

<Tip>
You can override the default averaging formula by enabling **Calculate Final Score based on Formula** on the **Appraisal Cycle** and entering a Python expression that references `goal_score`, `average_feedback_score`, and `self_appraisal_score`.
</Tip>

## Appraisal Overview report

The **Appraisal Overview** report (HR module → Reports) shows every employee's goal score, self score, feedback score, and final score side by side. Filter by **Appraisal Cycle**, **Department**, **Designation**, or **Company** to narrow the view. The bar chart at the top visualizes the top 10 employees by final score.
