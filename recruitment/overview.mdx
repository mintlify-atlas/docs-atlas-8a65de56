## How the recruitment pipeline works

Each stage produces a document that feeds into the next:

```
Job Requisition  →  Job Opening  →  Job Applicant  →  Interview  →  Job Offer  →  Employee
```

<Steps>
  <Step title="Raise a job requisition">
    A department head creates a **Job Requisition** specifying the **Designation**, **No of Positions**, **Expected Compensation**, and **Expected By** date. HR reviews it and sets the status to **Open & Approved** when the hire is greenlit.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create a job opening">
    HR creates a **Job Opening** — either directly or by clicking **Create Job Opening** on the approved **Job Requisition**. The opening is linked back to the requisition automatically and can be published to your website for public applications.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Receive and manage applicants">
    Candidates apply online or HR creates **Job Applicant** records manually. Each applicant is tracked with a status (**Open**, **Replied**, **Hold**, **Accepted**, or **Rejected**) and can be sourced from job boards, direct applications, or employee referrals.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Conduct interviews">
    From a **Job Applicant** record, HR schedules one or more **Interview** sessions against configured **Interview Round** definitions. Interviewers submit **Interview Feedback** with skill ratings and a result of **Cleared** or **Rejected**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Extend a job offer">
    For applicants who clear all interview rounds, HR creates a **Job Offer** document with offer terms, compensation details, and a letter head. The offer is submitted and the status is tracked as **Awaiting Response**, **Accepted**, or **Rejected**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create the employee record">
    Once a **Job Offer** is accepted, click **Create Employee** to generate a new **Employee** record pre-populated from the offer and applicant data. The hire is complete.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Supporting documents

| Document | Purpose |
|---|---|
| **Staffing Plan** | Defines planned vacancies per designation for a date range; validates that job openings and offers stay within approved headcount |
| **Interview Round** | Reusable template that sets default interviewers, expected skill set, and expected average rating for a type of interview |
| **Job Opening Template** | Reusable template for populating job opening descriptions quickly |
| **Employee Referral** | Lets employees formally refer candidates; automatically creates a **Job Applicant** and tracks referral bonus eligibility |

## Recruitment analytics

The **Recruitment Analytics** report (HR → Reports → Recruitment Analytics) provides a hierarchical view linking each **Staffing Plan** to its **Job Openings**, each opening to its **Job Applicants**, and each applicant to their **Job Offer** with offer date and status. Filter by **Company** and **Date** to see a snapshot of the entire pipeline at a point in time.

<Note>
Frappe HR enforces that a job applicant cannot be created against a closed **Job Opening**. Ensure openings are set to **Open** before accepting applications.
</Note>
