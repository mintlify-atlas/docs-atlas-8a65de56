Gratuity is an end-of-service benefit paid to employees upon separation. Frappe HR calculates gratuity based on the employee's years of service and a configurable **Gratuity Rule**.

## Gratuity Rule

A **Gratuity Rule** defines the calculation method. Go to **Payroll > Gratuity Rule > New**.

### Fields

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| **Calculate Gratuity Amount Based On** | *Current Slab* — apply the rate of the slab that matches total experience. *Sum of all previous slabs* — sum contributions from every slab up to the employee's total experience. |
| **Work Experience Calculation Method** | *Round off Work Experience* — round years to the nearest integer. *Take Exact Completed Years* — use the floor of decimal years. *Manual* — enter years of service manually on the gratuity document. |
| **Total Working Days Per Year** | Days per year used to convert total days worked into years (default: 365). |
| **Minimum Year for Gratuity** | Employees must complete at least this many years to be eligible. |
| **Applicable Earnings Component** | The salary components (from the last salary slip) whose amounts are used as the base for gratuity calculation. |
| **Current Work Experience (slabs)** | Table defining the fraction of applicable earnings per slab: **From Year**, **To Year**, **Fraction of Applicable Earnings**. Set both to `0` for an unlimited slab. |

### Example: Indian gratuity (Payment of Gratuity Act)

| From Year | To Year | Fraction |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | 0 | 0.0192 |

- **Method**: Sum of all previous slabs
- **Total Working Days Per Year**: 26 (as per the Act)
- **Minimum Year for Gratuity**: 5
- **Applicable Component**: Basic Salary

This gives: `Basic × (total_working_days / 26) × 0.0192`, equivalent to `(Basic / 26) × 15 × years_of_service`.

## Gratuity calculation formula

For the **Current Slab** method:

```
Gratuity = total_applicable_earnings × experience_years × fraction_of_applicable_earnings
```

For the **Sum of all previous slabs** method:

```
Gratuity = Σ (slab_years × total_applicable_earnings × fraction_of_applicable_earnings)
```

Where `total_applicable_earnings` is the sum of the **Applicable Earnings Component** amounts from the employee's most recent submitted salary slip.

Work experience is computed as:

```
work_experience = (relieving_date - date_of_joining - non_working_days) / total_working_days_per_year
```

Non-working days are LWP days (if **Payroll Based On = Leave**) or absent days (if **Payroll Based On = Attendance**).

## Creating a gratuity document

Go to **Payroll > Gratuity > New**.

### Fields

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| **Employee** | The separating employee. The employee must have a **Relieving Date** set. |
| **Gratuity Rule** | The rule to apply for this employee |
| **Posting Date** | Date of the gratuity document |
| **Current Work Experience** | Auto-calculated from joining and relieving dates (or entered manually) |
| **Total Amount** | Auto-calculated gratuity amount (read-only) |
| **Pay via Salary Slip** | Enable to disburse through a salary slip; disable to make a direct GL entry |
| **Payroll Date** | (Pay via Salary Slip) The payroll cycle date for disbursement |
| **Salary Component** | (Pay via Salary Slip) The earning component used for the payout |
| **Expense Account** | (Direct payment) Account to debit the gratuity expense |
| **Payable Account** | (Direct payment) Account to credit the gratuity liability |
| **Mode of Payment** | (Direct payment) Payment method |
| **Cost Center** | Cost center for the accounting entry |

<Note>
The employee must have a submitted salary slip in the system. The gratuity amount is calculated from the applicable component amounts in the **last submitted salary slip**.
</Note>

## Submitting and disbursing

<Steps>
  <Step title="Save and review">
    Save the gratuity document to trigger automatic calculation of **Current Work Experience** and **Total Amount**. Review the computed values.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit">
    Click **Submit**.
    - If **Pay via Salary Slip** is enabled, an **Additional Salary** record is created automatically. The gratuity amount appears in the employee's salary slip for the specified **Payroll Date**.
    - If **Pay via Salary Slip** is disabled, GL entries are posted immediately: the **Expense Account** is debited and the **Payable Account** is credited.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Record payment (direct payment only)">
    After submitting without salary slip, create a payment entry against the gratuity document to mark the liability as paid.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Gratuity statuses

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| *Draft* | Not yet submitted |
| *Unpaid* | Submitted; payment not yet received |
| *Paid* | Submitted; paid amount equals total amount |
| *Cancelled* | Cancelled; GL entries reversed |

## Integration with Full and Final Statement

When processing an employee's Full and Final (F&F) settlement, the gratuity document links to the settlement workflow. The F&F process pulls outstanding gratuity amounts and includes them in the final payment calculation along with leave encashment, pending expense claims, and any other dues.

Ensure the gratuity document is submitted before initiating the Full and Final Statement so the amount is included in the settlement.
